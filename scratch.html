Training Priorities

Below is a simple training structure that balances technique, sparring, and conditioning for Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu.

  1. Warm-up (10–15 minutes)
    • Joint mobility & light stretching
    • Solo drills (shrimping, bridges, hip escapes)
    • Technical movements (shot entries, sprawls)
  2. Positional Drilling (20–30 minutes)
    • Specific positions (closed guard, half guard, turtle)
    • Top and bottom reps with clear goals
  3. Sparring & Situational Rounds (20–30 minutes)
    • Short rounds from focused positions
    • One or two full rounds to finish

Focus on clean technique first, then increase intensity and speed as you get comfortable.
